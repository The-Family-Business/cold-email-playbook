# Cold Email for Marketing Agencies: New Client Acquisition Guide

Marketing agencies have a predictable problem: they're good at getting results for clients but inconsistent at getting clients for themselves. Cold email, done well, fixes that.

## Your ICP as an Agency

You're not targeting "all businesses." You're targeting businesses:
- In industries where you have case studies
- At revenue scales that can afford your retainer
- With founders or marketing leaders who make the decision
- Showing signals of growth (new funding, new hires, expanding)

## Lead Sources

**Google Maps** — great for local businesses: restaurants, retail, professional services, contractors. They need marketing but often don't have internal capability.

**LinkedIn** — better for SaaS, mid-market companies, businesses where the decision-maker is a VP of Marketing or CMO. Use BYOK Apify in Suplex.

## The Pitch That Works

Don't pitch your services. Pitch a specific observation about their business.

"Your Google reviews mention [specific thing]. Most [industry] businesses ignore that — it's actually a retention and referral goldmine. We've helped [similar client] turn their review strategy into 30% of new business."

That's not a template. It's research turned into relevance.

## Volume Strategy

Agencies need 2-5 new clients/month to grow healthily. At a 2-5% reply rate and 20-30% close rate on calls:
- 200 emails/month → 4-10 replies → 1-3 new clients
- 1,000 emails/month → 20-50 replies → 4-15 new clients
