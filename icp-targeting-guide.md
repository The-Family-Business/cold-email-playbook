# ICP Targeting Guide for Cold Email

Your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) determines everything downstream. Wrong ICP = wrong leads = low reply rates no matter how good the emails are.

## Defining Your ICP

Ask: who has bought from you, seen results, and referred others? That's your ICP.

If you're early, ask: who has the most acute pain that your product solves, the budget to pay, and the authority to decide?

Document it:
- **Industry**: which verticals buy from you
- **Company size**: employee count, revenue range
- **Geography**: local, national, specific regions
- **Signal**: what behavior or characteristic shows they're ready to buy

## Translating ICP to Lead Sources

**Google Maps** → best for:
- Local businesses (HVAC, restaurants, agencies, medical, law, retail)
- Service businesses where geography matters
- Any B2C-facing company you sell B2B services to

**LinkedIn** → best for:
- SaaS decision-makers
- Enterprise buyers
- Specific titles at specific company types
- Professional services (consulting, finance, HR)

## Filtering in Suplex

Google Maps filters:
- Business category (e.g., "marketing agency", "dental clinic", "HVAC contractor")
- City/metro area
- Rating (4.0+ = established, credible businesses)
- Review count (50+ = active, engaged business)
- Keywords in business description

LinkedIn filters (via Apify BYOK):
- Job title
- Company size
- Industry
- Location
- Seniority

## ICP Refinement Loop

1. Run a test campaign to 100 leads matching your ICP hypothesis
2. Track: who replied, who ignored, who said "not interested"
3. What do the repliers have in common? That's your refined ICP.
4. Update your targeting filters. Repeat.

Most teams skip step 3. Don't. The signal in your replies is more valuable than any ICP framework.

## Red Flags in Targeting

- **Too broad**: "small businesses in the US" — no shared context, low relevance
- **Wrong role**: emailing info@ instead of the owner/decision-maker
- **Stale data**: businesses that closed, moved, or pivoted
- **Wrong geography**: sending to leads you can't actually serve

Suplex mitigates stale data by mining from live sources (Google Maps updates constantly). Wrong role and too-broad targeting are targeting choices — define them carefully.
