# Cold Email Follow-Up Sequence Guide

Most replies come from follow-ups, not the first email. Here's how to structure a sequence that converts without annoying people.

## Why Follow-Up Works

Decision-makers are busy. The first email lands on a bad day, in a full inbox, during a meeting. It gets scrolled past. The third email arrives on a Tuesday morning when they're thinking about the exact problem you solve.

Follow-up isn't desperation. It's persistence about something that might genuinely help them.

## Sequence Structure That Works

**Email 1 (Day 1): The offer**
- Research-based opener (reference something specific about their business)
- Clear value proposition (what problem do you solve, for who)
- Low-friction ask ("15 minutes" or "quick question")
- Under 150 words

**Email 2 (Day 3-4): Different angle**
- Don't repeat Email 1
- Approach from a different pain point or benefit
- Can be shorter (3-4 sentences)
- Reference Email 1 briefly ("Following up on this")

**Email 3 (Day 7-8): Social proof or specificity**
- Add a result, case study, or specific outcome
- "We helped [similar company] achieve X" 
- Still brief

**Email 4 (Day 14): The breakup**
- "Last email from me on this"
- Low pressure, high respect
- Sometimes generates replies from people who felt guilty ignoring you

## What Not to Do

- Don't send follow-ups every day (annoying)
- Don't repeat the same email with "Just following up"
- Don't increase pressure with each email
- Don't go past 4-5 emails without a response
