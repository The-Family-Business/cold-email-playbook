# Google Maps Lead Mining: The Free Alternative to Apollo

Apollo charges $99/month for lead data. Google Maps has more local business data — and it's free.

Here's how to mine it systematically for B2B prospecting.

## Why Google Maps Works for B2B

Every local business has a Google Maps profile. That profile contains:

- **Business name** and category
- **Website URL** (the most valuable field)
- **Phone number**
- **Rating** (filter for quality businesses)
- **Review count** (proxy for business size/activity)
- **Customer reviews** (use for personalization)
- **Hours** (signals if they're active)
- **Address** (for local targeting)

For B2B services targeting local businesses — agencies, law firms, dental practices, restaurants, contractors, real estate companies — this beats any paid database.

## The Mining Workflow

### 1. Define Your Search

Be specific:
- Business type: "digital marketing agency"
- Location: "Chicago, Illinois" (or any radius)
- Filters: minimum 4 stars, 50+ reviews, has website

### 2. Extract the Data

Manually: time-consuming but free

### 3. Enrich with Website Analysis

Once you have the Google Maps data, analyze each website:
- What does the company do?
- What technology do they use?
- What problems do their customers mention in reviews?

Suplex runs this enrichment automatically, locally, on your machine — no per-record fees.

### 4. Find the Email

Options:
- Website contact form scraping
- Email pattern guessing + verification (first.last@domain.com)
- Email verification tools

Suplex handles email discovery and verification as part of the same pipeline.

### 5. Personalize Using Review Data

Google reviews are a goldmine. If a prospect's customers consistently mention "responsive service" and "quick turnaround," your cold email can reference the fact that their reputation is built on speed — and position your offer in that context.

Suplex extracts review themes and uses them as personalization variables in AI-generated email openers.
