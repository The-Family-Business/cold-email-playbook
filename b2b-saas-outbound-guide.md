# B2B SaaS Outbound: Cold Email Strategy for Software Companies

SaaS companies live and die by pipeline. Inbound SEO and content takes 6-12 months to mature. Paid acquisition is expensive and competitive. Cold email fills the gap.

## SaaS ICP for Cold Email

Your ICP is narrower than you think. Start with:
- **Title**: who actually makes the buying decision? (CEO at small co, VP/Director at mid-market, champion + committee at enterprise)
- **Company size**: where does your pricing fit? ($49/mo product ≠ enterprise sales motion
- **Tech stack**: what tools are they already using? Compatibility matters.
- **Behavior signals**: hiring for roles that indicate pain (hiring a data analyst = probably drowning in spreadsheets)

## The Outbound Sequence That Works for SaaS

**Email 1**: Pain-based opener + clear product positioning. "Companies using [competitor/tool] often struggle with [specific limitation]. [Product] solves this by [how]."

**Email 2**: Social proof. "We helped [similar company] reduce [metric] by [amount] in [timeframe]."

**Email 3**: Different angle. Feature they might not know about.

**Email 4**: Breakup. "Last email — if the timing is ever right, [link]."

## Metrics to Track

- Reply rate: 2-5% is healthy for SaaS cold email
- Demo booked rate: 20-35% of replies should book
- Close rate from demo: depends on product/market fit
- CAC from cold email vs other channels: compare monthly
